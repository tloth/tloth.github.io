Project overview

The product: Book Club web app for my book club to keep track of what books we have read and when

Project duration: March - April 2023 (design phase)

The problem: My book club does not have a single source of truth for which books we have read, when, whose choice it was and where we held the meeting

The goal: A web app to serve as an information hub for my book club with accurate statistics

My role: I worked on the design phase alone, including user research and UX/UI design

Responsibilities: user research, wireframing, prototyping, usability testing

Context for the book club

The book club I am in is a self-organised group of 3-6 people (currently 4). We take turns suggesting 3 books to read, from which the rest of the members vote democratically for one. We agree on a time and date to meet, this usually allows one or two months to read the book. All the organising happens in a WhatsApp group chat.

Understanding the user

User research summary

I conducted 15-minute interviews with 3 members of my book club. I wanted to understand why they are still in the book club after months/years as it is a community-driven club with no financial or other tangible incentive to read and discuss books other than a love for books and spending time together.

I am also a user as I am a member of the book club. I feel the need to save notes and quotes in one place that I can later go back to if I feel like it. I assumed this was a need we all shared, but the interviews showed only two other people shared this with me.

All people mentioned it being hard to look up a book we had read before if they can't remember the title or author.

There was a wide range of platforms users consume their books on: physical copies, e-readers, audiobooks (via an app or sometimes even youtube).

Pain points

  • saving notes and quotes in one place
  • looking up a book if we don't remember the title
  • not remembering whose turn it is to pick a book next
  • voting for a book
  • finding a time and date that works for everyone

Problem statement

Minna is a book club member who needs a way to organise and keep track of book club meetings and the books they read because she wants to keep a record of all the books and meetings in one place.

Starting the design

Competitive audit

I audited two book club apps, Bookclubs and Fable, and noticed both have a very simple colour scheme in order to let the book covers stand out.

Abandoned features

I did Crazy Eights exercises to come up with ideas for two problems: voting for a book from three options and finding a meeting time and date that works for everybody.

Using the voting feature in the Book Club app would require members to leave WhatsApp (where we currently handle all the organising), use the app, then come back to WhatsApp. This is way more hassle than using WhatsApp's new poll feature (or even just typing out the three options), so I decided to leave the voting feature out.

Finding a meeting time and date has been quite difficult in the group chat, however, moving this to the app would only make the process easier if everyone had an always up-to-date digital calendar that they wouldn't mind sharing, which I know for a fact not everybody does. Hence this feature was scrapped, too.

Sitemap

The first usability testing session showed that the initial low-fidelity digital wireframes based on this sitemap were highly confusing. What was the difference between a book and a meeting? Aren't they interconnected? So I started from scratch and created a new sitemap based on user stories.

User stories

  • I want to add a new book when the group decides what to read next
  • I want to create a new meeting when the group decides where and when to meet to discuss the book
  • I want to immediately see what the club is currently reading and the details of the next meeting
  • I want to take notes and save quotes from the book I'm currently reading
  • I want to see all the books the club has read
  • I want to filter and search the books the club has read
  • For each book, I want to see the details, my notes and rating, and meeting details in one place (members present, ratings, where and when)
  • I want to see all the current and past members of the club

These user stories were also super helpful when I wrote the tasks for the usability testing

Sitemap v2

I merged books and meetings to be one entity and removed the "Members" page as it did not serve a purpose wth the revised user stories.

Usability testing

Low-fidelity prototype on Figma

Study details

Study type: moderated usability study

Location: remote in the UK

Participants: 4 participants (all book club members or book lovers)

Length: 15-20 minutes

Tasks and questions were based on the User stories above

Findings

Affinity diagram with the findings from the first usability study

Themes

  • Search: timeline, tags

    • scroll and skim to find book
    • search for tags —> books need tags
    • search by date —> need visible timeline/dates

  • Confusion over

    • “Books”: need to make the scope of the app clear: book club only, not like goodreads
    • “Profile” Profile page should only contain account information; have a separate “My shelves” page for a personalised books page

  • Edit icon was not intuitive

  • Suggestions from users

    • want to see ratings (individual, average)
    • see other people's notes & quotes after the meeting

Insights

  • Users have different ways of searching for books in a list: search for keywords, search for dates, skim visually by scrolling. (Dates are already visible under each book. The covers are there so users can scroll and skim.)
  • Users get confused by the scope of the app: does it contain all the books they have read or only books the book club has read? (This should be clear once the app is populated. Book clubs only for now. Rename sections to make clearer.)
  • Users expect to find basic personal and account information on the "Profile" page
  • Users expect to find their personalised book collections on a different page, most commonly on “My shelves”
  • Users want to rate books and see other people's ratings
  • Users want to see other people's notes and quotes after the book club meeting

Actionable tasks based on insights (prioritised)

  • P0: Profile page should contain account information only. Navbar: rename “Books” to “Club books”, add “My shelves”, rename “Profile” to “My account”
  • P0: Create new “My shelves” page for personalised book lists (”Books I have read”, “Books I want to read”). Rename “Books I have read” to “Club books I have read”
  • P0: Rate book, see average rating for each book
  • P1: Add tags to books which you can then search for/filter books by (might be available via an API)
  • P2: Reveal everyone's notes and quotes on the book page after the meeting

Refining the design

Mockups

First of all, I created a sticker sheet with reusable components to make the process more streamlined and flexible.

I turned the most common components into Figma components for ease (navbar and CTAs).

I always keep accessibility in the forefront of my mind. I tested the colour contrast using WebAIM's contrast checker, and set the base/smallest font size to be 18pt. I made sure each interactive element has enough padding for bigger fingers or shaky cursors. I made each interactive element stand out so users can be certain about what they can click or tap. I will liaise with the developer to make sure that there is a logical flow for keyboard and/or screenreader users and that the headings are in a hierarchical order.

I used a Wacom digital tablet to create some simple illustrations (they are the book club members' favourite animals enjoying some literature!) to spice up the otherwise simple colour scheme and designs.

"My shelves" page

Renaming items for clarity: "Books I have read" becomes "Club books I have read", and "Books I want to read" becomes "Books I want us to read" to make the scope of this page clear

I designed for desktop and mobile screens: used the smallest possible frame for both. This will make it easier for developers to implement the designs. The grid I used also restricts the content to never overflow on huge screens and stay legible and skimmable to the human eye.

The second round of usability testing, where the tasks were the same as in the first round, was overall very positive, users found the flow intuitive and pleasant.

Some suggestions came up that I will definitely consider:

  • Fill up space on homepage with most used action (e.g. "Add quote")
  • Show who chose each book
  • Remove “Mark all as TBC” from "Add new book" page, default time, date and location to TBC
  • Make it clear which section is visible to everyone/only you
  • Move sort by and search under the “Currently reading” box
  • Refine sort by

Going forward

The idea for the book club app came from my friend, who is also a member. She is a software developer and cannot wait to start building the app!

  • Consider suggestions from last usability testing
  • Annotate the designs for the handover
  • Decide on undesigned interactions: how to upload photos? How to implement time, date and location pickers?
  • Design logged out state + sign-up/log-in flow

Learnings

  • I am now confident using Figma components thanks to creating the sticker sheet's reusable components
  • Using white space, especially on desktop, is a wholel art form which I found very difficult at the beginning, but I feel I am getting better at
  • Sometimes you have to throw out an idea and start from scratch. Building the lo-fi wireframes and realising they were very confusing for users was a valuable lesson not to get attached to anything
